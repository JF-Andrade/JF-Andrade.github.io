Summary

Digital marketing teams allocate millions in advertising spend across multiple platforms, but often lack rigorous methods to measure true ROI. Traditional last-click attribution fails to capture carryover effects (adstock), saturation, and cross-channel interactions.

This project applies Bayesian Media Mix Modeling using PyMC-Marketing to provide statistically rigorous ROI estimates with uncertainty quantification, optimal budget allocation recommendations, and multi-region analysis with hierarchical modeling across 18 territories and 8 currencies.

The project delivers a complete decision-support system: an interactive 6-page Streamlit dashboard featuring Budget Optimization for spend reallocation scenarios and Regional Analysis across 18 territories, plus 5 publication-ready visualizations with 94% HDI uncertainty bands and a fully reproducible ML pipeline tracked with MLflow.

The Business Challenge

Measuring the true impact of digital advertising requires solving three interconnected challenges that traditional attribution models ignore:

  • 01
    Carryover Effects (Adstock) Ads continue influencing conversions days or weeks after exposure. A customer who saw an ad today may purchase next month.
  • 02
    Saturation Effects Doubling spend does not double returns. Each channel has diminishing marginal returns at higher investment levels.
  • 03
    Cross-Channel Interactions Channels work synergistically. Google Search may convert customers who were first exposed via Meta awareness campaigns.

Ridge Baseline

  • Approach: Frequentist linear regression with L2 regularization.
  • Scope: Single region (UK only, GBP currency).
  • Runtime: ~15 minutes on CPU.
  • Limitation: No uncertainty quantification.

Hierarchical Bayesian

  • Approach: Full probabilistic model with PyMC.
  • Hierarchy: Currency → Territory → Channel (nested structure).
  • Runtime: 4-8 hours on CPU (Numba backend).
  • Advantage: Full posterior distributions for all parameters.

Methodology: Bayesian Media Mix Model

Revenue Decomposition

The model decomposes total revenue into attributable components:

Revenue = Base + Σ(Channel Effects) + Controls + Seasonality + ε

Adstock (Carryover)

GeometricAdstock with learned decay rate (α). Captures how ads continue to influence conversions over time.

  • α ~ Beta(2, 2) — Decay rate centered at 0.5
  • l_max = 12 — Maximum lag of 12 weeks

Saturation (Diminishing Returns)

LogisticSaturation models decreasing marginal returns. Hill curve with learned parameters.

  • L ~ HalfNormal(1.0) — Half-saturation point
  • k ~ Gamma(2, 1) — Curve steepness
