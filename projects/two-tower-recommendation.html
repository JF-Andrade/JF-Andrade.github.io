Summary

This project demonstrates the end-to-end engineering of a Production-Grade Recommender System capable of serving personalized content from a catalog of 105k+ items.

Moving beyond typical "competition notebooks," this solution treats Machine Learning as a Software Engineering discipline. It implements a robust Two-Stage Funnel Architecture, orchestrated by a reproducible MLOps pipeline with strict prevention of data leakage, hardware-accelerated training, and modern experiment tracking.

The Business Challenge

The goal was to predict exactly which 12 items a customer would buy in the next 7 days, maximizing MAP@12. This presents the "Trinity of Complexity":

  • 01
    Extreme Cold Start Hundreds of new items appear weekly with zero history.
  • 02
    High Seasonality Fashion preferences rotate rapidly.
  • 03
    Massive Scale \(1.37M \text{ users} \times 105k \text{ items} \approx 144 \text{ Billion pairs}\).

Solution Architecture

To solve the scale problem ($144B$ pairs), a "Funnel" strategy was implemented, inspired by architectures used at YouTube and LinkedIn.

Stage 1: Retrieval (Recall)

  • Model: Two-Tower Neural Network (TFRS)
  • Mechanism: Maps Users and Items into a shared 32-dim embedding space.
  • Key Feature: Visual Embeddings (ResNet50) to "see" product similarity.
  • Output: Reduces 105k items to top 100 candidates via BruteForce Indexing.

Stage 2: Ranking (Precision)

  • Model: CatBoost Classifier
  • Why: Superior handling of categorical features.
  • Context: Uses interaction history, retrieve scores, and user metadata.
  • Output: Re-orders candidates to generate the final Top 12.

Benchmark: Production vs. Kaggle Gold

Kaggle Gold winners achieved superior performance by leveraging massive Ensembles (combining 6-10 distinct retrieval models) and aggressively retrieving 10x more candidates per user. While computationally expensive, this "accuracy-at-all-costs" approach sets the theoretical performance ceiling.

Production Strategy

Two-Stage Architecture

The developed solution implements a robust Two-Stage Architecture, combining a Two-Tower Retrieval model (Dual Encoder) built on TensorFlow Recommenders for candidate generation, with a high-performance CatBoost regressor for precise re-ranking. This design balances recall and precision, leveraging the Dual Encoder for efficient large-scale retrieval (using In-batch Negative Sampling) and Gradient Boosting for capturing non-linear interactions in the final ranking.

Pipeline orchestration is rigorously managed by a custom Python framework (run_pipeline.py) that enforces strict temporal partitioning integrity to prevent data leakage. It integrates seamless artifact management and experiment tracking via MLflow, prioritizing end-to-end local reproducibility and serving-readiness over theoretical complexity.

Kaggle Gold Strategy

Multi-Source Ensemble

Leading solutions outperform this architecture primarily by adopting Multi-Source Recall strategies and Model Ensembles. While this project relies on a single candidate source (Two-Tower), winners aggregate candidates from dozens of complementary heuristics—such as Item-Item Collaborative Filtering, Co-occurrence Matrices, and Trend Algorithms—capturing signals that deep neural networks might miss.

Furthermore, the use of dozens of Gradient Boosting models together (Stacking/Blending) reduces prediction variance, and feature engineering is significantly more granular, exploring direct comparisons between the candidate item and the user's immediate interaction history.
